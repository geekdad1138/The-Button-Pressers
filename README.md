# The Button Pressers

**The humans, the whims, and the accidental ripples that built the digital world.**

## The Vision
In "The First Clicks," we mapped the technical milestones. In "The Button Pressers," we look at the person in the chair. This project is about the moments of boredom, frustration, or pure "what if?" that triggered the massive shifts we live in today.

This isn't about the *code*; it's about the **moment the finger hit the key**.

## The Anatomy of a "Press"
Every entry in this repository avoids the academic. We want to know what it felt like to be there.

* **The Human:** Who were they at that exact moment? (An annoyed consultant, a bored student, a stubborn researcher?)
* **The Button:** The specific, small decision or action. Not a "5-year plan," but a single choice.
* **The Ripple:** How that one choice cascaded into a world-changing reality.

## The First "Pressers"
* **The Gamer (Ken Thompson):** Wanted to play a space game on a bad computer; accidentally wrote the foundation for the modern internet (UNIX).
* **The Namer (John McCarthy):** Needed a catchy title for a summer workshop proposal; accidentally branded an entire era of humanity (AI).
* **The Organizer (Tim Berners-Lee):** Just wanted to keep his own notes straight at work; accidentally wove the World Wide Web.
