# The Gambler

## **Jensen Huang (2006)**

### **The Human**

It’s 2006. Jensen Huang is the CEO of NVIDIA, a company known for making teenage gamers happy with faster graphics cards. But Jensen is restless. He has a hunch that the math required to draw a dragon in a video game (parallel processing) is the same math required to simulate the universe.

### **The Button**

He "presses the button" on **CUDA**. He decides to spend billions of dollars to turn every single NVIDIA gaming card into a general-purpose supercomputer. Wall Street hates it. Analysts call it a "waste of resources" that will tank the company. Jensen ignores them, betting the entire company's future on a use-case that doesn't even exist yet.

### **The Ripple**

For nearly a decade, CUDA was a "solution looking for a problem"—until the **Stubborn (Hinton)** and the **Believer (Sutskever)** realized that Jensen’s gaming chips were the only things on Earth powerful enough to train deep neural networks.

* **The AI Foundry:** Because of Jensen’s gamble, NVIDIA became the "arms dealer" for the AI revolution.
* **Modern Compute:** Every LLM on Earth is currently running on the "gamble" Jensen took in 2006. He turned a toy into a brain.
