# The Poet

## **Ada Lovelace: The Poetical Scientist (1843)**

#### **The Human**

Ada is the daughter of the "mad, bad, and dangerous to know" poet Lord Byron. Her mother, terrified Ada would inherit her father's "insanity," forces her to study mathematics and logic from a young age to "tame" her imagination. But Ada doesn't see math as a cage; she sees it as a language. When she meets Charles Babbage and sees his "Analytical Engine," she doesn't just see a calculator—she sees a collaborator.

#### **The Button**

While translating a French paper about the engine, Ada decides to add her own "Notes." In **Note G**, she "presses the button" on the world's first true algorithm: a step-by-step set of instructions for the machine to calculate Bernoulli numbers. More importantly, she makes a choice to look past the gears. She writes that the machine could process *anything* that follows rules—music, art, or logic—not just numbers.

#### **The Ripple**

The machine was never built in her lifetime, but her "button press" survived in the archives.

* **The First Programmer:** She defined the logic of loops and variables 100 years before a digital computer ever turned on.
* **Universal Computation:** She was the first person to realize that a computer is a **symbol processor**, not a number cruncher. Every time an AI "writes" a poem or "composes" a song today, it is fulfilling the "Poetical Science" vision Ada had in a London study in 1843.
