# The Gamer

## **Ken Thompson: The Three-Week Space Game (1969)**

#### **The Human:**

It’s 1969. Bell Labs just pulled the plug on a giant, expensive operating system project. Ken Thompson is a guy who just wants to play a game he wrote called *Space Travel*. He has a wife and a young son, and his wife is away on vacation for three weeks.

#### **The Button:**

With his wife away, Ken has a quiet house and a scavenged PDP-7 computer. He decides to spend those three weeks seeing if he can write a "cleaner" system just to run his game. He spends one week on the operating system, one week on the shell, and one week on the editor.

#### **The Ripple:**

He didn't know he was building an empire. Because he built it to be "simple" and "portable" (so he could keep playing his game), it became **UNIX**. It survived because it was easy to move to other machines. Today, if you use an iPhone (iOS), a Mac (macOS), or a server (Linux), you are running code that exists because a dad had a quiet three weeks and a space game to play.
