### **Linus Torvalds: The "Just for Fun" Explosion (1991)**

#### **The Human**

It’s 1991 in Helsinki, Finland. Linus is a 21-year-old computer science student. He’s frustrated because the academic operating system he’s using (Minix) is limited, and the professional version (UNIX) is way too expensive for a student. He’s not trying to start a revolution; he’s just a kid who wants his new 386 PC to do more than it currently can.

#### **The Button**

On August 25, 1991, Linus posts a message to a newsgroup. He "presses the button" on **vulnerability**. He admits he’s working on a free operating system, famously calling it **"just a hobby, won't be big and professional like gnu."** He invites people to tell him what features they want. He didn't hide his code away until it was perfect; he threw the doors open while it was still a mess.

#### **The Ripple**

That "hobby" became **Linux**.

* **The Internet Engine:** Today, Linux runs 100% of the world's top 500 supercomputers, the vast majority of the internet's servers, and every Android phone.
* **The Open Source Logic:** By admitting it was "just for fun," he lowered the barrier for others to help. He pressed the button on the **Collaborative Era**, proving that a distributed group of volunteers could out-build a multi-billion dollar corporation like Microsoft.
