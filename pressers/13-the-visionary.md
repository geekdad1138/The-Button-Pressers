# The Visionary

## Doug Engelbart (2018/2020)

### **The Human**

It’s 1968. Most people see computers as cold, giant calculators used by governments to crunch numbers. Doug Engelbart, a soft-spoken engineer, sees them as a way to "augment human intellect." He believes computers should be a bicycle for the mind—interactive, visual, and collaborative.

### **The Button**

On December 9, 1968, Doug takes the stage in San Francisco for what is now called **"The Mother of All Demos."** He "presses the button" on **Interactivity**. He shows off a wooden box with two wheels (the first mouse), hypertext, multiple windows, and even real-time video conferencing—concepts that wouldn't become "standard" for another 20 to 30 years.

### **The Ripple**

Doug didn't just show off gadgets; he shifted the definition of a "user."

* **The Interface:** Every time you click a link, move a mouse, or join a Zoom call, you are living inside Doug’s 1968 demo.
* **Human-Machine Symbiosis:** He pressed the button on the idea that computers aren't for replacing people, but for making us smarter together.
