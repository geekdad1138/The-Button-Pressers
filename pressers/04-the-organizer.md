# The Organizer

### **Tim Berners-Lee: The Digital Rolodex (1980/1989)**

**Filename:** `pressers/04-the-organizer.md`

#### **The Human**

Tim is a young software consultant at CERN, the massive physics lab in Switzerland. He is overwhelmed. CERN has thousands of people, hundreds of projects, and dozens of incompatible computer systems. Tim isn't trying to change the world; he is just trying to remember who is in charge of which vacuum pump.

#### **The Button**

To solve his own messy desk problem, he writes a small program called **ENQUIRE**. The "button press" was his choice to use **hypertext**—links that could go anywhere. He didn't build a hierarchy; he built a web. He later refined this into a proposal that his boss famously labeled as "Vague but exciting."

#### **The Ripple**

That "Vague" proposal became the **World Wide Web**.

* **The Permissionless Link:** By deciding that any page could link to any other page without asking for permission, he pressed the button on the global democratization of information.
* **The Universal Interface:** He transformed the computer from a calculation tool into a window. We aren't "calculating" right now; we are "browsing," a concept born from Tim’s need to organize his office contacts.
