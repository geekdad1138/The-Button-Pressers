### **Geoffrey Hinton: The Thirty-Year Bet (2012)**

#### **The Human**

It’s the early 2000s. The "AI Winter" is in full effect. The world has moved on to "Expert Systems" and "Support Vector Machines." Geoffrey Hinton is a British-Canadian researcher who is being told, repeatedly and politely, that his obsession with **Neural Networks** is a dead end. He is seen as a relic of a failed 1950s dream.

#### **The Button**

Despite the lack of funding and the skepticism of his peers, Hinton "presses the button" on **persistence**. He refuses to pivot. He spends decades refining the math of **Backpropagation**—the engine of neural networks—waiting for the rest of the world (and the hardware) to catch up to his vision.

#### **The Ripple**

In 2012, his team enters the **ImageNet** competition. Using the "old" neural network ideas he refused to abandon, powered by modern GPUs, they crush the competition.

* **The Big Bang:** This single win proved Hinton was right all along. It triggered the "Deep Learning" revolution.
* **The Modern World:** Every face-recognition software, self-driving car, and LLM exists because one man was too stubborn to admit his favorite idea was "obsolete."
