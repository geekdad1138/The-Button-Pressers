# The Admiral

## **Grace Hopper: The "Grandmother" of the Bug (1947)**

### **The Human**

It’s 1947 at Harvard. Grace Hopper is a math genius and a US Navy officer working on the Mark II computer. In an era where "programmers" were often seen as mere clerical workers, Grace was a visionary who believed that computers should be able to understand human language, not just binary math. She was frequently told "that's not how computers work."

### **The Button**

One hot September afternoon, the Mark II stopped working. Grace and her team traced the error to a literal **moth** trapped in a relay. She didn't just throw it away; she taped the moth into the team’s logbook and labeled it **"First actual case of bug being found."** She "pressed the button" on the **Language of Computing**—deciding to treat the machine as something that could be "debugged" and spoken to in English.

### **The Ripple**

Grace’s insistence that humans shouldn't have to speak "machine" led to the creation of **COBOL**, the first widely used high-level programming language.

* **The Software Bridge:** She created the first "compiler"—the tool that translates human words into machine code.
* **The Vocabulary:** Every time a developer today says they are "debugging code," they are using the terminology Grace coined while holding a moth in a Harvard basement. She pressed the button on **Human-Centric Programming**.
