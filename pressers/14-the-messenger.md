# The Messenger

## **Claude Shannon (1948)**

### **The Human**

Claude Shannon is a quiet, unpretentious mathematician at Bell Labs who likes to ride a unicycle and juggle while he thinks. While everyone else is worried about how to make telephone wires clearer or vacuum tubes faster, Claude is thinking about the very nature of "meaning" itself.

### **The Button**

In 1948, he publishes "A Mathematical Theory of Communication." He "presses the button" on the **Bit**. He decides that all information—whether it’s a Shakespeare sonnet, a photo of your dog, or a voice over a wire—can be stripped of its meaning and reduced to a simple choice between 0 and 1.

### **The Ripple**

By separating the *message* from the *medium*, he created the digital age.

* **The Digital Language:** He gave us the math to compress data and correct errors. Without the "Bit," there is no internet, no streaming, and no AI.
* **Information Theory:** He proved that "Information" is a physical property of the universe, like energy or matter. He pressed the button on the **Digital Foundation**.
